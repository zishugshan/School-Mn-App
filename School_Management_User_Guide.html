1. Introduction

The School Management System is a web-based platform designed to streamline school administration. It provides different views and capabilities based on user roles: Super Admin, School Admin, Teacher, Student, and Parent.

Key Features

  • Student and teacher record management
  • Class and section organization
  • Attendance tracking
  • Homework assignment and submission
  • Test and examination management
  • Marks and grading
  • Timetable scheduling
  • Library management
  • Built-in chat
  • Reports and analytics
  • Bulk data import

2. Getting Started

Accessing the Application

Open your browser and navigate to the school's URL. You will see the login page.

Logging In

  2. Enter your password.

3. Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is your home screen after login. It displays:

  • Quick Stats — Total students, teachers, attendance percentage, upcoming events.
  • Upcoming Tests — Tests scheduled in the next few days.
  • Recent Activity — Latest attendance records, homework submissions.
  • Notifications — Unread alerts and reminders.
  • Charts — Attendance trends, performance graphs (varies by role).

Each role sees a customized dashboard. Admins and teachers see school-wide statistics. Students see personal attendance and homework. Parents see their child's performance.

4. Student Management

Viewing Students

Navigate to Students in the sidebar. The table shows Name, Email, Class-Section, Roll Number, Gender, and Parent Name. Use the search bar to find a student.

Adding a Student

  1. Click Add Student.
  2. Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email, Date of Birth, Gender, Class, Section, Phone, Address.
  3. Click Create.

Default password: password123.

Editing a Student

Click on any student row to view details, then click Edit to modify.

5. Teacher Management

Viewing Teachers

Navigate to Teachers. The table shows Name, Email, Employee Code, Qualification, Specialization, Phone, and Status.

Adding a Teacher

  1. Click Add Teacher.
  2. Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email, Qualification, Date of Birth, Gender, Phone, Address.
  3. Click Create.

6. Class & Subject Management

Navigate to Classes to view all classes (1–12) with sections A, B, C. Navigate to Subjects to view subjects taught. Subject-to-class mappings determine availability per grade level.

7. Attendance

  1. Navigate to Attendance.
  2. Select a Class and Section.
  3. Select a Date.
  4. Mark each student as: Present (green), Absent (red), Late (yellow), or Leave (blue).
  5. Click Save.

Students view their attendance in My Attendance. Teachers see class-wise summaries. Admins see school-wide analytics.

8. Homework

Creating Homework

  1. Navigate to Homework and click Add Homework.
  2. Enter Title, Description, Subject, Target Class/Section, Due Date, and Max Score.
  3. Click Create.

Student View

Students see pending assignments under My Homework.

Teacher Review

Teachers can view submissions and provide marks and feedback.

9. Tests & Marks

Creating Tests

  1. Navigate to Tests and click Add Test.
  2. Enter Title, Subject, Class, Section, Date, Maximum Marks, Passing Marks, and Exam Type.
  3. Click Create.

Entering Marks

  1. Navigate to Marks and select a test.
  2. Enter marks for each student and click Save.

Leaderboards

View top-performing students across classes and subjects under Leaderboards.

10. Exams & Timetable

Exam Schedules

Navigate to Exams to view upcoming exams with title, subject, class, date, time, and room.

Timetable

Navigate to Timetable and select a Class/Section to view 6 periods per day, Monday–Friday, with subject, teacher, and room for each period.

11. Library

Navigate to Library to browse books and digital resources organized by category. Books show title, author, ISBN, publisher, and availability. Digital resources include PDF notes and online learning links.

Books are automatically issued to students matching their grade level with issue date, due date, and status tracking.

12. Chat

  1. Navigate to Chat.
  2. Search for a user by name or email.
  3. Click on a user to open a conversation.
  4. Type your message and press Enter or click Send.

Conversations auto-refresh every 5 seconds. Unread message count appears on the sidebar.

13. Reports & Analytics

Reports — Generate student performance reports, attendance summaries, and class-wise analysis.

Analytics — Visual data insights including attendance trends, test score distributions, subject-wise comparisons, and demographic data.

14. Notifications

Click the bell icon in the top bar to view notifications about homework, tests, attendance updates, and announcements.

15. Bulk Import

Available to Super Admins. Navigate to Import.

Importing Students

Upload CSV/Excel with columns: First Name, Last Name, Email, Date of Birth, Gender, Class, Section (Address, City, State, Pin Code optional).

Importing Teachers

Upload CSV/Excel with columns: First Name, Last Name, Email, Date of Birth, Gender, Qualification, Address, Phone.

Default password for imported users: password123.

16. Profile & Settings

Profile — View and edit personal information, name, email, contact details, and class/section (for students).

Settings — Update notification preferences and change password.

18. FAQ & Troubleshooting

Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?

Contact your school administrator to reset your password.

Q: Why can't I see certain menu items?

Each role has specific permissions. Refer to the Role-Based Access table above.

Q: The student I added doesn't appear in the list?

The list shows up to 200 records sorted by newest first. Use the search bar to find specific students.

Q: How do I change my class or section?

Contact a School Admin or Super Admin to update your assignment.

Q: How often does the chat refresh?

The chat checks for new messages every 5 seconds automatically.

Q: What browsers are supported?

Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions).
